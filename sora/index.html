Project Background

The Master's program in Human-Computer Interaction (MHCI) Carnegie Mellon University allows students to work in teams with an industry sponsor (in my case, The Boeing Company) to implement an iterative research/development/design process.


This project began in January 2014 and will be completed in August 2014.


We are currently researching new methods to streamline communication at Boeing factories, improving efficiency and safety.

Current Summary Report

Team Name, Logo, and Design Language

Our team first brainstormed a team name and logo, which took multiple reiterations of conceptual ideas. Our team name Sora means "sky" in Japanese. The consistency of our branding identity was important, so we determined our typography, layout, colors, and styles for collateral.

Research

To expand our domain knowledge and determine our problem scope, we are reading research papers, interviewing factory workers, and obtaining insights from workers in analogous domains. Over two trips in February 2014, we travelled to the Boeing factories in Seattle and Renton to gather user data from factory workers. We developed 12 key insights based off our notes and are currently continuing to consolidate our data.
